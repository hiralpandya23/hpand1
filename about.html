Biography

Albert Einstein (14 March 1879 – 18 April 1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who is widely regarded as one of the greatest scientists in history. He is best known for his theory of relativity and his famous equation E=mc², which describes the relationship between mass and energy.

Einstein was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect. He immigrated to the United States in 1933 to escape Nazi persecution and became a professor at Princeton University, where he spent the rest of his life.

Accomplishments

  • Theory of General Relativity
  • Special Theory of Relativity
  • Explanation of the Photoelectric Effect
  • Development of Quantum Theory
  • Contributions to Statistical Mechanics

Personal Life

Einstein was married twice and had three children. He was an avid violin player and enjoyed sailing and hiking. He was also a pacifist and a supporter of civil rights, and spoke out against racism and anti-Semitism.
